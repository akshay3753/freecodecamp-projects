Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion lives

Here's a timeline of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1914 - Born in Cresco, Iowa
  • 1933 - Leaves his family's farm to attend the University of Minnesota
  • 1935 - Pauses school, joins Civilian Conservation Corps
  • 1937 - Graduates and joins US Forestry Service
  • 1938 - Marries Margret Gibson; returns to study pest-resistant plants
  • 1941 - Rejected from military, works on scientific projects for war effort
  • 1942 - Receives Ph.D. in Genetics and Plant Pathology
  • 1944 - Moves to Mexico to lead plant pathology program
  • 1945 - Doubles wheat yields by growing wheat twice a season
  • 1953 - Creates a strain of wheat that provides 95% of Mexico’s supply
  • 1962 - Brings high-yield wheat to India and Pakistan
  • 1970 - Wins the Nobel Peace Prize
  • 1983 - Helps seven African nations improve grain production
  • 1984 - Becomes professor at Texas A&M University
  • 2005 - Advocates for GM crops to feed the growing population
  • 2009 - Passes away at the age of 95

"Borlaug's life and achievement are testimony to the far-reaching contribution that one man's towering intellect, persistence and scientific vision can make to human peace and progress."
